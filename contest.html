BUSMASTER is an Open Source Software tool to simulate, analyze and test data bus systems such as CAN.

 

Download, run, study, redistribute and modify. Join the BUSMASTER community!

Coding Contest 2014


Description

The coding contest is designed to encourage and inspire students, freelancers, and programmers to begin participating in the open source development of BUSMASTER, help identify and bring in new contributors, and provide students interested in computer science and other fields with exposure to real-world software development, including coding work, documentation and open source community involvement.

 

The contest will consist of a 1-month registration phase running April 1 – 30, 2014 and a 3-month development phase running May 1 – July 31, 2014.

How to apply

To apply for the contest, developers have to send in their entries to busmaster@etas.com during the registration phase. You also have to provide access to your source code repository and have to sign and send in the Contributor Agreement. We appreciate to receive a short biography.

 

At the end, a committee of ETAS and RBEI colleagues will evaluate which applications are eligible for the contest and inform you.

How to win

During the development period you implement your feature. Once you finish, send this information to busmaster@etas.com.

 

At the end, a committee of ETAS and RBEI colleagues will evaluate all entries and decide on the winners. The first winner receives a cash price of €7000, the second winner receives €2000, and the third winner receives €1000. The top 10 participants will be interviewed and honored as part of BUSMASTER promotion activities.

Interesting features

The project's issue tracker on GitHub already contains some features interesting for the community. They are labelled as "1.1 major improvement" or "1.2 minor improvement".

 

Major features include new bus systems, such as

  • Support of bus system Ethernet (#52), or
  • Provide multi-platform support (#45).

 

Minor features include new transport protocols, such as

  • Support of transport protocol XCP/CCP (#43),
  • Support of transport protocol CANopen (#40), or
  • Direct DBC support (#586).
